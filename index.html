artist statement (Dec 07 2024)

Whenever there’s a political turmoil breaking out somewhere else on the map, seeing people put it on their Instagram stories with the red heartbreak emoji, he feels an uncontrollable joy, followed by an almost tragic sense of absurd relief. It's not cynicism, he insists. It's sorrow. Immense and honest. Maybe even resilient.

For them, bad news always comes from afar. One more calamity, he thinks, and the distance between them and him might grow smaller. The barriers thinner. But the actual distance—between people and people—is immutable.

Years ago, on R’s Twitter homepage, there was a photo: Serge Gainsbourg and Jane Birkin running toward the camera, hands clasped. His idealized image of being in love.

He drinks Fiji water. Thinks Kanye West is phenomenal. Reaches into the pocket of his Belgian designer coat. Finds a cigarette. Plays a post-Soviet rock album.

The sky is clear. Not freezing. Frost on car windows. He thinks it'll all melt—traceless—within the hour. J’s demo is still playing. It was just a gesture. A shared delusion.

“I want to write like Ingeborg Bachmann one day,” he thinks—until J's voice interrupts: soft, like a sweet AI named Emily. Downloadable only with a paid subscription.

He imagines becoming a novelist over the next thirty years. Remembers a lecture from Orhan Pamuk: it takes 100 years for a village to become a city in New York. 50 in Istanbul. 20 in Shanghai.

Yesterday, he returned to Shanghai via Mapillary. Google Street View doesn’t work. He wandered virtual streets, jumping seasons with clicks. Someone once asked him to describe Shanghai. They expected a village. It used to be true. But not anymore. Not with a Balenciaga billboard.

Still, they call it the French Concession. A symbol of superior taste. A fantasy everyone fills with their own flâneur. Bourgeois classiness in a communist country. Colonial bittersweet, he thinks. They’ll never get it.

Everything is accelerating.
Everything becomes flatter.
He could not sleep.
Gentrification is underrated, he thinks.
